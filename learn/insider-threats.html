Insider Threats

An insider threat is a security risk that originates from within the targeted organization. It typically involves a current or former employee, contractor, or business partner who has authorized access to the network and misuses that access to compromise data or systems.

Types of Insider Threats

Insider threats are not always malicious. They can be broken down into two main categories:

  • Malicious Insiders: These are individuals who intentionally steal data, sabotage systems, or commit fraud for personal or financial gain, revenge, or ideological reasons.
  • Negligent or Unintentional Insiders: These are individuals who accidentally expose the organization to risk, often through carelessness, falling victim to a phishing attack, or failing to follow security policies.

How to Mitigate Insider Threats

  • Implement the Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP), ensuring users only have access to the data they absolutely need.
  • Conduct regular security awareness training for all employees.
  • Monitor user activity on critical systems to detect anomalous behavior.
  • Establish clear and efficient offboarding procedures to immediately revoke access for departing employees.
