Password Security

Passwords are the primary gatekeepers to our digital lives. Ensuring their strength and uniqueness is one of the most critical steps in personal cybersecurity. A weak or reused password can be the single point of failure that leads to a catastrophic data breach.

Creating a Strong Password

A strong password is one that is difficult for both humans and automated computer programs to guess. The key principles are length, complexity, and unpredictability.

  • Length: Aim for a minimum of 12-15 characters. The longer the password, the exponentially harder it is to crack.
  • Complexity: Use a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols (e.g., !, @, #, $).
  • Unpredictability: Avoid using common dictionary words, sequential numbers (12345), or personal information like your name, birthday, or pet's name.

Password Best Practices

  • Use a unique password for every single account. Reusing passwords means that if one site is breached, attackers can access all your other accounts.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) whenever it is offered. This adds a crucial second layer of security.
  • Use a reputable password manager to generate and securely store long, complex, and unique passwords for you.
  • Change passwords immediately if you suspect an account has been compromised.
