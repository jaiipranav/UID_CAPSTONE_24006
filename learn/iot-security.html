IoT Security

Internet of Things (IoT) security is the practice of protecting internet-connected devices and the networks they're connected to from threats and breaches. IoT devices include everything from smart home appliances and wearables to industrial sensors and medical devices.

Common IoT Vulnerabilities

Many IoT devices are not designed with security as a priority, leading to common weaknesses.

  • Weak or Default Passwords: Many devices ship with simple, default credentials that users never change.
  • Lack of Updates: Manufacturers often fail to provide regular security patches for their devices, leaving known vulnerabilities unpatched.
  • Insecure Network Services: Open ports and insecure services on the device can be easily exploited.
  • Unencrypted Data: Sensitive data is often transmitted and stored without proper encryption.

How to Secure IoT Devices

  • Always change the default username and password immediately upon setup.
  • Keep device firmware and software updated. Disable features you do not use.
  • Place IoT devices on a separate Wi-Fi network (a guest network) to isolate them from your primary computers and phones.
  • Research a device's security features and manufacturer's reputation before purchasing.
