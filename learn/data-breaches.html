Data Breaches

A data breach is a security incident in which sensitive, protected, or confidential data is copied, transmitted, viewed, stolen, or used by an individual unauthorized to do so.

Common Causes

Data breaches can happen for a variety of reasons, ranging from targeted attacks to simple human error.

  • Weak or Stolen Credentials: Attackers often use stolen usernames and passwords to gain access to systems.
  • Malware and Phishing: Malicious software can exfiltrate data, and phishing attacks can trick employees into revealing sensitive information.
  • Insider Threats: A current or former employee may intentionally or unintentionally expose data.
  • Physical Theft: The theft of laptops, external drives, or paper records can lead to a data breach.

How Organizations Prevent Data Breaches

  • Data Encryption: Encrypting data both at rest (on servers) and in transit (over the network).
  • Access Control: Implementing the principle of least privilege, ensuring employees can only access the data they absolutely need.
  • Employee Training: Educating staff on security best practices, including how to spot phishing attempts.
  • Regular Security Audits: Proactively searching for and patching vulnerabilities.
